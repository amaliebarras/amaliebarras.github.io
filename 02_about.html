amalie barras

data / design in Boulder, CO

I love data because it helps us to understand the truth in the world around us.

Knowing this, I started my career as a data analyst. I loved it, actually. I could have analyzed data and delivered reports for my whole life and it would have been great, but I started to notice that my work was losing context and impact, the more it was handed off. I realized that data alone wouldn't change the world, it would require a new skill to translate that data into behavior change. Over time I began to realize that they had a word for this process -- design.

After exploring a few different areas of design on my own and then doubling down on my design education, I was eager to apply these skills to yummy social and community challenges. I did so though projects in our local Code for America brigade, and then served as a research-focused UX Designer in the Design, Tech, and Innovation Fellows program at the City of Austin.

I came to Boulder, Colorado in 2017 and I now work as a UX Designer & Researcher at an accelerator inside Cognizant, bringing ideas to life by defining and designing software MVPs.

I frame problems with research and testing, and design and optimize solutions with iterative, agile methods.
